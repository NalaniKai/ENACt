Sponsors

We thank our sponsors who pledge to donate their time and resources to various challenges and causes.

E.L Realty Advisers of Connecticut

A sponsor of over 100 challenges, E.L Realty Advisers has helped many groups challenge each other. Whether it be in the form of donation matching or the donation of time, E.L Realty Advisers has always been there to help. Most recently, E.L Realty Advisers donated two equal checks of over five thousand dollars to two elementary school’s light bulb drive in an effort to replace the schools’ incandescent bulbs with high efficiency compact fluorescent light bulbs.

LRS Services

As a new sponsor, LRS Services has not held back from getting involved. One of their first contributions came in the form of hauling services. Two local companies had a cardboard recycling challenge, and over several hundred pounds of cardboard was collected. LRS Services offered to pay for all hauling of cardboard to the local recycling facility.

Bank of Wilton

A sponsor form the beginning, Bank of Wilton has always been there to help people finance their various projects that are centered on environmental awareness. We thank Bank of Wilton for their continued support of all individuals wanting to make a change.

Profiles of the month

Here is a collection of our favorite profiles this month. These profiles continue to contribute to environmental awareness, through their donation of various resources or participation in environmentally friendly acts.

Realty Juggler

This past month Realty Juggler volunteered over 50 hours of community service at their community’s local park. The group helped remove litter, planted trees around the perimeter and inside the park, and helped install new recycling and trash receptacles throughout the park. Realty Juggler has two ongoing challenges including a bike to work challenge and a lowest power bill challenge. In the past Reality Juggler has participated in a total of seven challenges, and is always looking for another challenge.

BioNex

Just last week, BioNex donated 30 large steel recycling bins to a local high school. The group saw a lack of recycling occurring in their local school district and decided they would help out. BioNex is currently participating in no challenges, but is eager to join one. BioNex is concerned with electronic recycling and water quality. In the past BioNex has participated in over 20 challenges.

Trilogy Excursions

Last year, Trilogy Excursions collected over one ton of marine trash floating around the waters of Maui County (Maui, Molokai, and Lanai). The group provides tourists with a boat and snorkel tour around reefs surrounding Maui, and invites their guest to collect as much marine trash as possible during their snorkeling tour. Trilogy Excursions continues with their original mission of cleaning up the reefs and waters of Maui County, and hopes to collect two tons of trash by the end of 2016.
